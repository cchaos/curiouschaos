Spheric

Spheric is a social platform created for collegiate level eSport players. The client came to me with a logo, color scheme, and basic wireframes. My job was to create a living style guide and build the static web pages.

Mockups to Reality

The client previously hired a UX team to conduct user research, create user stories, and create wireframes. These wireframes were then passed to me to use as a guide for the user flows and layout. Most of the time I agreed with the ideas within the wireframes, but there were times where I needed to alter the design based on my own UX knowledge.

Atomic Design

I am a big proponent of Atomic Design, originally advocated by Brad Frost. Atomic Design is the idea of organizing front-end materials (design systems) starting with the fundamentals and building upon those to become more complex.

In his explanation he utilizes the following terminology: Atoms > Molecules > Organisms > Templates > Pages. However, I don't think the biology terms necessarily translate well for everyone. The terminology I've standardized in my own work is: Core > Elements > Fixtures > Templates > Pages.

Responsive

One of the challenges – and delights – was deciding how to make the entire application feel more native on mobile devices. Utilizing Angular's custom routing and HTML fragmenting features, I moved any secondary navigation – like the sidebar – into it's own view on mobile devices to mimic navigation systems like those of messaging apps.
