cinar.io

WILL Interactive is a creator of interactive training films. They realized that, from the 20+ years of film making, they were accumulating a large library of video snippets that could be utilized by other companies or individuals in their own training. My job was to take this concept of selling single and branching training scenarios and make it a reality in just under two months.

Personas

WILL employed a marketing company to conduct initial market research and potential user surveys. Based on these findings, we were able to group users into two categories â€“ individuals and publishers. Individual users are most likely HR professional who create their own training materials and are looking to supplement these with more engaging media. Publishers are companies who create and sell off-the-shelf training products and also want to find captivating media.

Vallery Smith

Director of Training - Human Resources Department

  • Works at a company of 10,000 employees
  • Company spends more than $100,000 in training per year
  • Researches and recommends content but may not actually buy
  • Creates internal course - wants to add pre-made assets
  • Usually does use video in training programs
  • Prefers interactive over linear content but may purchase linear if no interactive ones suit needs
  • Has seen this type of non-linear content before

VALCOM

Publisher of Resale Training Programs

  • Wants to re-sell / put into their own resale training

The publisher persona was still a big unknown, so we decided to concentrate on the individual user while utilizing a few callouts to speak to the publishers.

Minimal Viable Product

We knew that the process of creating this website was going to be a process. We didn't have prior knowledge of the particular market or how it would react to this type of offering. Therefore, we knew needed to get something online to be able to start testing with real users and get real feedback.

Style Guide

I created the style guide to make sure we were approaching the design in a way that aligned with our potential users mindset. It gives the right logo, colors, fonts. and images to use, but why.

Final

In addition to user experience responsibilities, I was also the primary designer and front-end developer.
